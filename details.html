Model performance

Computing…

Calibration: chronological 80 / 20 split. Held-out events become positive labels (awake); synthetic negatives are placed half a fitted period earlier (the antipodal phase, expected to be sleep). Each held-out timestamp is predicted using only training events. Brier score is mean squared error of probability vs label — lower is better.

Caveat. Predictions reflect when Edson has been observed awake, not direct evidence of his sleep itself. So an "asleep" verdict literally means "the recent wake phase of the cycle is far from this query phase" — not that we have evidence he is asleep. Far from recent data, the model abstains: low confidence is a real answer, not a loading state.
